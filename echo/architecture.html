Sources → memory → skills → you.

Five ways to draw the same architecture: your data flows in from many sources, gets filed into one unified, wiki-style memory, and a macro orchestrator runs skills over it — acting through tools you switch on and grow. Same content every time; different shape and emphasis.

Rough on purpose — this is about which structure reads best, not the visuals. Tell me which one (or which mix) and I'll make that one beautiful. Your live How-it-works page is untouched.
Variant 1

The Stack — top-down architecture

The literal layer cake, read top to bottom. Inputs at the top, your unified memory as the load-bearing middle, the orchestrator reading it and dispatching skills, everything acting through tools, and you at the base. Closest to the reference diagram — clearest for "how is this built."

Variant 3

Concentric — you at the centre

On-brand with the Echo rings. You and the orchestrator at the core, wrapped by the skills, wrapped by your memory — with sources feeding in from the left and tools reaching out to act on the right. Best for the "it all revolves around you" feeling, less for build detail.

You're the centre of gravity: signal pulls inward through memory and skills to Echo, then pushes back outward through tools into action.

Variant 4

Memory at the centre — the hub

Puts the unified wiki in the middle as the thing everything connects to. Sources pour in from the left; the orchestrator sits above maintaining it; skills sit below reading and writing it; tools extend right to act. Best for arguing "the memory is the product."

Variant 5

The Story Strip — one real week

Not an architecture at all — a narrative. Follow a single concrete example through every layer, panel by panel. Best for a landing page or a pitch, where you want someone to feel it before they see the box diagram. Pairs well with any of the four above.

1 · Sources arrive

Monday night: your stand-up transcript, a calendar invite for Friday, your Fitbit logs three poor nights, an email from Sarah.

→
2 · Filed into memory

Facts land in your wiki: Sarah → birthday next Wed. Health → sleep trending down. Calendar → 4pm flight Friday.

→
3 · Echo connects it

The orchestrator reads across entries: travel Friday + poor sleep + Sarah's birthday all collide this week. It decides what matters.

→
4 · Skills act

Health: nudge earlier nights. Scheduling: hold a packing slot Thursday. Life: a gift idea for Sarah.

→
5 · Tools + you

Drafts the gift email, pencils the calendar holds, and pings you on Telegram: "here's your week — approve?"

One Tuesday, end to end — the same five layers, but told as something that happened to you rather than a system you have to understand.
