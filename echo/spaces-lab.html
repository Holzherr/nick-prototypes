Spaces · concept

One you. Many Spaces.

How Echo holds Personal, Family and Business as separate, sovereign Spaces — yet shares a module across them by reference (one source of truth, not a copy) and rolls the bits you care about into one dashboard for you. Two ways to draw it.

Like Notion workspaces, but each Space is its own private store — federated, never pooled. Sharing is a permission on a module, not a duplicate.
Variant A

The Venn — shared by reference

Three Spaces, each owning its own modules. Where they overlap, a module is shared (view / contribute / manage) — the same database, seen from two Spaces. You sit at the centre: the one identity who belongs to all of them.

Personal — your VM Family — shared (the Family Space) Business — the company's

Reads as: each Space is sovereign and private; an overlap is a module deliberately shared between them — never a second copy.

Variant B

The "Me" lens — it all rolls up to you

The other half of the story: your three Spaces stay separate, but a personal Me lens composes the slices you care about from each into one dashboard. A view, not a merge — you choose what surfaces here.
