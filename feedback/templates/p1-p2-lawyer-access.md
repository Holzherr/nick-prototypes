## Why this is two prototypes

GitLaw has two candidate ways for users to get legal help, at opposite ends of the build-difficulty axis:

| | Prototype | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| 🟢 Easiest to ship | **P1 — Lawyer loop-in (BYO)** | The user invites their own lawyer as a Guest. No marketplace, no vetting. |
| 🔴 Hardest to ship | **P2 — Legal-advice agent** | Pure AI agent. UPL, accuracy, trust are load-bearing. |

We want a lawyer's gut reaction on **both**, and which problem-space we should bet on first.

---

## What to do

1. Open **P1**: https://holzherr.github.io/nick-prototypes/lawyer-loop-in-lite/b-byo.html
2. Click through — pretend you're the *user* inviting their lawyer, then the *lawyer* receiving the invitation. The right column shows the lawyer's view.
3. Open **P2**: https://holzherr.github.io/nick-prototypes/legal-advice-agent/
4. Try asking the agent the kinds of questions a non-lawyer founder might ask ("is this term normal?", "should I push back on indemnity caps?").
5. Form a comparative view: which would your clients actually use? Which would *you* be comfortable having out there with your name attached?

---

## What we want feedback on (UX-focused)

### P1 — BYO

1. **Lawyer onboarding** — when you (as the lawyer) get the invite, is it clear what you're being asked to do? Where would you hesitate?
2. **Scope clarity** — review only? Take over? Sign off? Where does the boundary get fuzzy?
3. **Liability comfort** — where does professional liability sit? Anything missing you'd need before accepting?
4. **Pricing model intuition** — what should the user pay? What should the lawyer get?

### P2 — AI agent

5. **Trust** — where does the agent feel reliable vs risky? Specific examples are gold.
6. **UPL boundary** — agent is framed as advice, not legal opinion. Is the framing clear enough? Where does it cross a line?
7. **Escalation handoff** — when the agent offers to escalate to a human lawyer, does it pass enough context?
8. **Substitution risk** — would your clients use this *instead of* calling you for the kind of work you actually want to be doing? Or only for noise you're glad to be rid of?

### Cross-cutting (P1 vs P2)

9. **Which bet first?** If you had to pick one to ship in the next 6 months, which and why?
10. **What would make you trust the agent (P2) enough to default away from the human flow (P1)?**
