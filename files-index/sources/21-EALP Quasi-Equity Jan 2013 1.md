## BETWEEN:

1. **FOODIENT LIMITED** a company registered in England and Wales with registered number 08001091 and whose registered office is at Birmingham Science Park Aston Faraday Wharf, Holt Street, Birmingham B7 4BB (the "**Company**"); and

2. **EARLY ADVANTAGE LIMITED PARTNERSHIP** registered in England and Wales as a limited partnership with registered number LP013679 and whose principal place of business is at Cavendish House, 39-41 Waterloo Street, Birmingham B2 5PP ("**EALP**") acting by its general partner Millpoint Limited.

## RECITAL

(A) This quasi-equity agreement ("**Agreement**") is in addition to the quasi-equity agreement entered into between the parties, under which EALP advanced £12,498.25 to the Company ("**Prior Quasi-Equity**").

(B) The Prior Quasi-Equity Agreement was made in accordance with the provisions of the subscription agreement (the "**Subscription Agreement**") made between: (1) the Company; (2) the Managers; (3) BCC; (4) EALP; (5) the Fund Manager; and (6) the Private Investors (as of which are as defined in the Subscription Agreement).

(C) EALP has agreed to advance a further £37,400 in accordance with the terms of this Agreement.

## IT IS AGREED AS FOLLOWS

### 1. Definitions

1.1 In this Agreement the following expressions shall have the following meanings unless inconsistent with the context:

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| "Business Day" | any day (other than Saturday) on which clearing banks are open for normal banking business in sterling in the city of London; |
| "Quasi-Equity Sum" | the sum of £37,400 or such part thereof as may be from time to time outstanding; and |
| "Interest Rate" | 8% per annum gross amount. |

1.2 Clause and Schedule headings do not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.

1.3 The content of the Recitals shall be deemed to form part of this Agreement.

1.4 Any terms defined in the Subscription Agreement shall have the same meaning in this Agreement (including the Recitals).

### 2. Interest and Repayment

2.1 The Quasi-Equity Sum shall be advanced by EALP to the Company by direct bank transfer on the date hereof.

2.2 The Quasi-Equity Sum shall bear interest from the date hereof at the Interest Rate accruing on a monthly basis on the outstanding capital element of the Quasi-Equity Sum. Where part of the Quasi-Equity Sum is repaid, the interest calculation will account for the capital repayment as if it had been received at 12 am the next day after its receipt into EALP's bank account.

2.3 No repayments of the Quasi-Equity Sum together with interest shall be due until such time as: (1) the Prior Quasi-Equity has been repaid in full; and (2) the quarterly EBITDA of the Company exceeds £50,000.00 (the "**EBITDA Target**"). From the date that the Company has repaid the Prior Quasi-Equity and achieves the EBITDA Target the Company will begin to make quarterly repayments of the Quasi-Equity Sum together with accrued Interest at the lower of £12,501 per quarter or 25% of the EBITDA per quarter.

2.4 All payments by the Company to EALP under this Agreement shall be net, after the deduction of any tax that the Company is required to deduct by law provided:-

  2.4.1 that any taxation deducted shall be and is paid to the relevant tax authority by its due date, or if payment is made by a deduction from other tax payments that such deduction is in a manner prescribed in law and the payment of the tax paid is made by the due date;

  2.4.2 EALP will issue an invoice to the Company detailing the interest charged on the Quasi-Equity Sum under this Agreement. The Company shall check the calculation of the taxation contained therein and shall pay the tax to the relevant tax authority by the due date and also issue to EALP a certificate of tax deduction in the form approved by the relevant tax authority within 7 days after payment of the taxation to the tax authority. In the event that EALP have not calculated correctly the taxation on its invoice, the Company shall immediately inform EALP of the same and pay the correct amount of taxation to the tax authority by the due date. EALP will issue an amended invoice in due course;

  2.4.3 the method of payment of interest shall be at the sole discretion of EALP and it may require such change as it determines on reasonable notice; and

  2.4.4 the Company shall provide EALP and/or the Fund Manager with any information it reasonably requires from time to time necessary for the correct calculation of the tax to be deducted from interest payments. The Company remains solely responsible for paying the amount of tax to the taxation authority.

2.5 Unless otherwise agreed in writing, all payments shall be made by the Company by direct bank transfer into a UK bank account nominated by the Fund Manager.

2.6 No amount repaid to EALP will be available for redrawing by the Company.

2.7 The Company may repay the Quasi-Equity Sum only as provided in this Agreement.

2.8 Subject to a minimum repayment of £5,000, the Company may prepay at any time without penalty or charge part or all of the Quasi-Equity Sum provided that all arrears and accruals of interest referred to in **clause 2.2** and all repayments of capital referred to in **clause 2.3** and all other sums owing to EALP and the Fund Manager under this Agreement and the Subscription Agreement have been paid up to date.

### 3. Events of Default

3.1 The following shall be events of default by the Company:

  3.1.1 if the Company fails to make payment to EALP or the Fund Manager within 14 days of any payment falling due in accordance with this Agreement or the terms of the Subscription Agreement (excluding the monthly monitoring fee payable to the Fund Manager under the Subscription Agreement);

  3.1.2 any warranty given by the Company and/or the Managers (as defined in the Subscription Agreement) in the Subscription Agreement is or proves to have been incorrect such that EALP is entitled to make a claim or claims under the terms of the Subscription Agreement;

  3.1.3 any other indebtedness of the Company becomes due or capable of being declared due prior to its stated maturity, or any security created by the Company becomes enforceable;

  3.1.4 the Company suspends or ceases to carry on its business or disposes of the whole or a substantial part of its business (in one or a series of related transactions) or substantially changes the nature of its business;

  3.1.5 the Company is adjudicated or is found to be insolvent or becomes unable to pay its debts within the meaning of the Insolvency Act 1986;

  3.1.6 an encumbrance is created other than by EALP or an encumbrancer takes possession of or a receiver or administrative receiver is appointed over any of the assets or part of the undertaking of the Company or a petition is presented for the appointment of an administrator under the Insolvency Act 1986;

  3.1.7 any distress, execution or other process is levied or issued upon the property or assets of the Company;

  3.1.8 any step is taken with a view to a composition, assignment or arrangement (within the meaning of the Insolvency Act 1986) with any creditors of the Company or any of its subsidiaries;

  3.1.9 any step is taken with a view of appointing an administrator (within the meaning of the Insolvency Act 1986);

  3.1.10 if an application (which is not frivolous or vexatious) is made to the court for an order for the winding up of the Company;

  3.1.11 it is or becomes unlawful for the Company or any of its subsidiaries to perform any of the obligations under the Subscription Agreement;

  3.1.12 this Agreement ceases to be binding or enforceable against the Company and/or any of its subsidiaries which is a party thereto or does not confer the security expressed to be created thereby;

  3.1.14 if EALP cease to be a shareholder of the Company for whatever reason; or

  3.1.15 if there is a change of control of the Company (as control is defined in section 1124 of the Corporation Tax Act 2010).

3.2 In the event of a default as specified in **clause 3.1**, EALP and/or the Fund Manager shall be entitled by notice in writing to the Company:

  3.2.1 to declare that the Quasi-Equity Sum and interest and all other sums payable pursuant to this Agreement are immediately due and payable and the Company shall immediately pay the same to EALP; and/or

  3.2.2 specify that henceforth the Quasi-Equity Sum is repayable on demand and upon any such demand being made for payment of any monies, such monies shall become payable immediately following such demand.

### 4. Undertaking

The Company undertakes that without the prior written consent of EALP, such consent not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed, it will not create any lien, charge or encumbrance over any of its property or assets.

### 5. General

5.1 The Company shall not be entitled to withhold or delay payment of any sum hereunder or in relation to any such sum exercise any right of set-off or counter claim.

5.2 The provisions of this Agreement shall be enforceable by and enure for the benefit of any successor in title to EALP.

5.3 Where any powers or rights are exercisable by EALP they will be exercised on its behalf by the Fund Manager.

5.4 Any release of any kind or compromise or any other arrangement of any kind by EALP or the Fund Manager shall not affect the rights and remedies of the party concerned as regards any other party or its rights and remedies against the party in whose favour the release waiver compromise or other arrangement is granted or made except (in any event) to the express extent of the release waiver compromise or other arrangement and no such release waiver compromise or other arrangement shall have effect unless granted or made in writing.

5.5 Each provision of this Agreement is severable and distinct from the others. The parties hereto intend that each of the provisions herein shall be and remain valid and enforceable to the fullest extent permitted by law. In the event that any provision herein (for any reason) is or becomes to any extent whatsoever invalid, illegal or not enforceable at law, it shall to that extent be deemed not to form part of this Agreement but save for the aforementioned limitation, it will continue to remain in full force and effect. All other provisions of this Agreement shall remain in full force and effect save where the operation of any such clause would negate the commercial intent or purpose of the parties hereto.

5.6 If any provision of this Agreement is illegal or unenforceable because of any measure or qualification or exceeds that permitted by law, the provision shall be deemed to have been modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid, effective and acceptable and enforceable in law subject to that modification not negating the commercial intent or purpose of the parties hereto.

5.7 The Company acknowledges that it has had the opportunity to obtain independent legal advice on this Agreement and has taken all necessary steps to ensure that it understands the full force and effect of the terms of this Agreement.

5.8 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with English Law and the parties irrevocably submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the English Courts.
