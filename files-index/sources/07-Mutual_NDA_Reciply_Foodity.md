# OPERATIVE PROVISIONS 

1.  **<span class="underline">The Confidential Information</span>**

<span class="underline"></span>

**“Confidential Information”** means all information whether commercial,
financial, technical or otherwise including without limitation all
secret or confidential information of the Disclosing Party and its
affiliates in whatever form, together with all analyses, compilations,
data, studies or other documents prepared by the Receiving Party which
are derived from or in connection with such information or which contain
or are based in whole or in part upon such information and if disclosed
in a tangible form must be conspicuously marked as being confidential
and if disclosed orally must be clearly indicated as being confidential
at the time of disclosure and confirmed as such in writing within ten
(10) days of disclosure. However, any information that Disclosing Party
may disclose to Company that Company may otherwise obtain hereunder
regarding either (i) Disclosing Party’s existing or potential customers;
or (ii) business plans, sales forecast or financial information, shall
be Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information whether or not it was
indicated as such at the time of disclosure, and if it is orally
disclosed, whether or not it is subsequently confirmed as such in
writing. For the purpose of this Agreement, “affiliates” means the
ultimate holding company of the Party in question, and any direct or
indirect subsidiary of such ultimate holding company.

2.  **<span class="underline">Disclosure of Confidential
    Information</span>**

<span class="underline"></span>

1.  The parties acknowledge that the Confidential Information is a
    valuable, special and unique asset belonging to the Disclosing
    Party. The parties agree that it will not disclose the Confidential
    Information to any person, firm, corporation, association or any
    other entity for any reason or purpose whatsoever except as provided
    in this Agreement.

2.  The parties shall be permitted to disclose the Confidential
    Information on a need-to-know basis to their officers, employees,
    affiliates, and if required, to their respective strategic partners,
    contractors and professional advisers (individually and collectively
    “Partner(s)”), provided always that, where Confidential Information
    is disclosed to a Partner such Partner signs an undertaking to treat

4.  The parties agree not to issue or release any articles, advertising,
    publicity or other matter relating to this Agreement or mentioning

    Business Purpose except as is provided herein.

5.  **<span class="underline">Return of Confidential

<span class="underline"></span>

The Disclosing Party may request in writing at any time that any written
Confidential Information and any copies of such shall be returned by the
Receiving Party with a written statement to the effect that upon such
return the Receiving Party has not knowingly retained in its possession
or under its control, either directly or indirectly, any Confidential
Information or copies of such. The Receiving Party shall comply with any
such request within seven (7) days of receipt of such request. Any part
of the Confidential Information which consists solely of analyses,
compilation, studies or other documents prepared by the Receiving Party
and which the Receiving Party by mutual agreement with Disclosing Party
does not return to Disclosing Party will be destroyed at the request of
Disclosing Party, and such destruction will be confirmed to Disclosing
Party in writing.

6.  **<span class="underline">Excluded Information</span>**

<span class="underline"></span>

The obligations in this Agreement shall not apply to any Confidential
Information that:

1)  is in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to the receipt
    from the Disclosing Party;

2)  is or becomes publicly known, otherwise than as a consequence of a
    breach of this Agreement;

3)  is developed independently by the Receiving Party;

4)  is disclosed by the Receiving Party to satisfy legal demand or order
    by a competent court of law or governmental body, or the rules or
    requirements of any regulatory authority or stock market by whose
    rules the Receiving Party is bound, provided however that in these
    circumstances the Receiving Party shall insofar as it is able advise
    Disclosing Party prior to disclosure so that Disclosing Party has an
    opportunity to defend, limit or protect against such production or
    disclosure, and provided further that the Receiving Party will
    disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information which is
    required by law or regulation to be disclosed and the Receiving
    Party will at Disclosing party’s direction and expense exercise all
    reasonable efforts to obtain a protective order or other reliable
    assurance that confidential treatment will be accorded to any
    Confidential Information required to be disclosed;

5)  is disclosed to a third party pursuant to written authorisation from
    Disclosing Party;

and

6)  is received from a third party without similar confidentiality
    restrictions and without breach of this Agreement.
